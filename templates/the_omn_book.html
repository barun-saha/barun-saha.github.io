Opportunistic Mobile Networks: Advances and Applications is our forthcoming book to be published by Springer. This book, #TheOMNBook, if you may say, presents a thoughtful assimilation of the recent advances in OMNs across several dimensions such as, routing, cooperation, heterogeneity, emotions, and more. The book also reflects on different real-life aspects and standards such as, Requests For Comments (RFCs) and patents. A dedicated chapter on network simulation is aimed to help you develop and test protocols with confidence. Additionally, the book also focuses on a few contextual topics in closely related domains, wherever relevant.

Key Benefits

In Opportunistic Mobile Networks: Advances and Applications, you will learn how to:
  • Resolve some of the key challenges in OMNs
  • Master protocol development and testing using the ONE simulator
  • Consider likely influences of human aspects in OMNs
  • Overcome the issues of non-cooperative behavior
  • Address heterogeneity in OMNs and other networks
  • Advance the current state-of-the-art in OMNs along different avenues

The primary objective of #TheOMNBook is to elucidate some of the fundamental challenges in OMNs, and guide you along different paths towards addressing such issues. The chapters have been crafted in a way so that both beginners as well as seasoned researchers would find them useful. These chapters are accompanied with a set of review terms and conceptual as well as programming exercises. Our hope herein is that #TheOMNBook would not only help you gain clear understanding of the fundamental concepts, but also establish you on a firm ground to further extend the contemporary state-of-the-art in OMNs.

The Foreword to the book has been written by Prof. Prasant Mohapatra, University of California, Davis; Editor-in-Chief, IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing. Prof. Jon Crowcroft, University of Cambridge, UK, and Prof. Marco Conti, Institute of Informatics and Telematics (IIT), National Research Council of Italy (CNR), Italy, have endorsed the book. The book has been co-authored by Sudip Misra and Sujata Pal. Opportunistic Mobile Networks: Advances and Applications would be available in print as well as electronic versions in March 2016.
